Newton's Cradle

Newton's Cradle is a device that demonstrates the conservation of momentum and energy. It consists of a series of equally spaced, identical balls suspended by strings. When one ball on the end is lifted and released, it strikes the next ball, transmitting the force through the series of balls and causing the ball on the opposite end to swing upward. This device illustrates the principles of Newton's Third Law of Motion: "For every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction."

Real-time application:

Newton's Cradle is often used as a desk toy and educational tool, but its principles are applied in various fields. In engineering, the conservation of momentum and energy is crucial for designing collision safety mechanisms in vehicles. In physics, the same principles help in understanding particle collisions. The device also finds applications in fields like robotics and material science where understanding impact forces and energy transfer is essential.
